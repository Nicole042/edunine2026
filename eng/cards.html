Full Paper Formatting Guidelines

The Full Paper Format is intended for mature, well-developed research results. This format applies to:

  • Category 1: Teaching Techniques, Classroom Experience Reports, Pedagogical Tools
  • Category 2: New Curricula and Educational Program Proposals
  • Category 3: Educational Research
Required Format
  • Abstract: max 150 words
  • Length: 4–6 pages
  • Template: IEEE format
  • English: formal academic style
Work in Progress (WiP) Guidelines

Work in Progress Papers are for ongoing projects with preliminary results or early-stage research. Applies to:

  • Category 1: Teaching Techniques, Classroom Reports, Pedagogical Tools
  • Category 2: New Curricula and Program Proposals
  • Category 3: Educational Research
Required Format
  • Title: Start with “Work in Progress:”
  • Abstract: max 150 words
  • Length: 2–4 pages
  • Template: IEEE format
  • English: formal academic style
Doctoral Symposium Guidelines

Venue for doctoral students to share work in progress and receive feedback. Required for:

  • Category 4: Research-in-Development (RID)
Required Format
  • Title: Start with “DS:”
  • Abstract: max 150 words
  • Length: up to 4 pages
  • Template: IEEE format
  • English: formal academic style
Workshop Proposal Guidelines

Workshop Papers describe interactive, hands-on sessions for skill-building and engagement. Applies to:

  • Category 5: Workshop Proposals
Formatting
  • Title: Start with “Workshop:”
  • Abstract: max 150 words
  • Length: Full Paper (6 pages) or Short Paper (2 pages)
  • Template: IEEE format
  • English: formal academic style
